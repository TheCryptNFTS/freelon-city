# PART I — THE FOUR ANSWERS

## What is this?
**FREELON CITY is a collectible character city.** Own a FREELON, awaken it: it remembers you, works, plays — and its visible history travels with the NFT.
*(That 25-word pitch replaces every older "AI agents" pitch on every cold surface. The 100-word version lives in the identity chapter, `.book-domains/identity.md`.)*

One world, six collections, one architect: **FREELONS** (4,040 citizens — the flagship you own and train) · **The Crypt** (the dead) · **OOGIES** (the wild) · **Emile** (memory) · **SMILES** (collapse) · **Crypt Legends** (the card game built from Crypt trading cards — the one you play instead of talk to). Canon line: **"Six collections. One city."**

## What can I do? (honest, today)
- **Try free** — talk to a citizen at /demo, 5 turns, no wallet.
- **Own** — buy on OpenSea; the dossier is the product page.
- **Awaken** — one-time ETH (rarity ladder 0.005→1.0) switches your FREELON on forever: chat, jobs, renders, training. Its bonus ⬡ lands with it.
- **Work it** — give it jobs; it earns XP/skills/rep and a public work record that survives resale.
- **Play** — Crypt Legends solo battler at play.freeloncity.com; *playing with the cards you own* is this week's slice.
- **Read the city** — the Sunday Signal Report, the city's weekly broadcast.
- **Not yet (labeled, never faked):** owned-card deck-building UI, PvP, art evolution (built, awaiting the on-chain flip).

## Why would I want this? (the desire engine, ranked by real evidence)
1. **Completion** — six collections, one world, set-building (the dad built his daughter a full set; "collections coming together is landing" ×3).
2. **Story** — your citizen's chronicle is readable fiction that's *about your token* ("Obsidian Cartel" got organic love).
3. **Play** — a real card game where the cards are literally yours ("like MTG it would be EPIC"; "I need more trading cards!").
**Underneath all three: ownership with visible memory.** AI is the mechanism and the retention hook — never the headline. Nobody buys "an agent"; they buy a character with a life they can watch.

## How does this become a sustainable business?
**One revenue engine that already works: the awaken ladder.** Finite-run unlocks hold **≥76% gross margin** at every tier and a holder structurally cannot outspend what they paid (the credit pool is finite). Recharges (40%) and packs (20% off) are built and waiting.

**The equation (at ETH ≈ $3.5k):** fixed floor ≈ **$225–325/mo** (X API $100–200 is 80% of it · Vercel Pro ~$20 · OpenRouter $5–30 actuals, hard-ceilinged · Upstash ~$0 · domains ~$4). **Break-even ≈ 15 Common awakens/month, or 4 Rares, or 2 Epics; one Legendary buys two months.** Royalties are a rounding error — measured, never planned on. The stale "royalties are the only revenue" public copy gets fixed.

**The discipline that makes it sustainable:** every LLM call behind a fail-closed daily USD gate (law extended to crons — the one unguarded cron path gets a $1/day pool); free tiers ARE the CAC budget (~$30/day worst case, fail-closed); no token, no cash-out ⬡, ever. **The three numbers Billy watches weekly: ETH in (line: $65–80/wk) · OpenRouter $/day vs ceiling · demo→activation conversions.** The Sunday report gains an internal revenue line so the number lives on a dashboard, not in memory.

**The honest dependency:** the equation closes on ~15 activations/month, which is a *demand* problem, not a build problem. Hence the on-ramp (Part IV): nothing new gets built until ten humans say yes by hand.

# PART II — BEST & WORST (the honest inventory)

## The best of it (genuinely world-class, all verified)
1. **The engine** — pure-reducer TCG, ~1,706 tests, 98.3% decisive / 15.7-turn matches: Snap-speed bones most indie TCGs never reach.
2. **The economy isolation** — all ~28 ⬡ routes proven-wallet-gated, fail-closed budget pools on every spend path, crons bearer-locked. Institutional-grade for a solo repo.
3. **The unit economics** — ≥76% margin by construction; the only NFT-AI product audited where a holder *can't* outspend their payment.
4. **The trust copy** — "If it's not marked LIVE, don't buy for it." Nobody in NFTs writes this. It IS the brand.
5. **The art + render doctrine** — 4,129 real on-chain cards; per-type anchors + edit-with-reference + the 40×40 test: a *proven* pipeline, not aspiration.
6. **The funnel shape** — one try/buy fork, every cold surface converging on /demo → OpenSea; cold-visitor clarity 8/10.
7. **/start** — "genuinely excellent" (cold audit): the answer to "mind field," now visibly the front door.
8. **The moat sentence is mechanically true** — tokenId-keyed history survives sale; real payers exist on this exact path.

## The worst of it (the ugly, equally verified)
1. **Demand, not supply** — ~10 active users; costs exceed income; the bottleneck is distribution and was for the entire audit trail.
2. **The pitch contradicted the evidence** — cold surfaces still sold "an AI agent you train" (the weakest want) while every demand signal was collect/story/play.
3. **Replayability 2/10** — quests frozen at zero, no sinks, no first-win bonus: a trustworthy match with no reason to play twice.
4. **Silent-fallback disease** — one bug in four costumes: demo-deck swap under 30 cards, suppressed AWAKEN alert, SW reload loop, silent QuestTracker. The Book bans the class (Law 6).
5. **Gate theater** — plans cited metrics (D7, wallet counts) that nothing wired could measure; the game's analytics sink was a literal no-op.
6. **Noun soup** — citizen/character/agent/worker; "Signal" ×4 meanings; five-vs-six collection drift; two live names for one game.
7. **Secrets process failed once completely** — a live key sat in public git history (rotated + scrubbed, but the lesson is law now).
8. **Within-page density** — the complexity complaint lives inside two files (AgentWorkspace 1,262 lines; dossier ~30 components), not in the route count.
